Why Change?

🕌 Amanah - Responsibility

In Islam, we have a duty to Allah and to our community. We must be honest with money and decisions. This is called 'Amanah' - taking care of what has been entrusted to us.

💡 Clarity & Trust

Questions from members about money and management are normal. Clear answers build trust. Transparency means: everyone can see how money is used and what rules we follow.

📋 What is a VZW?

A VZW is a 'Non-Profit Association'. This means all money we receive (donations) must be used for the mosque's goals. No one can make profit. This is set by law.

The Journey to Transparency

Step 1: Listening & Understanding

Members ask questions about how money is used and how the mosque is run. This is healthy and normal. Asking questions helps the community grow stronger.

What is needed: Open minds, patience, and willingness to listen to everyone's concerns.

Step 2: Ongoing Dialogue

We hold conversations in WhatsApp groups and organize coffee meetings with the board. This demonstrates that everyone can speak and ask questions.

What is needed: Communication channels, regular meetings, and a board that actively listens.

Step 3: Legal Foundation Review

We review the bylaws (rules) of our VZW. These articles define how we operate. We verify everything aligns with the law.

What is needed: Knowledge of VZW law (Articles 11, 14, 15 and 33 of our bylaws) and legal insight.

Step 4: Formal Procedure Launch

Members can request an 'Extraordinary General Meeting'. This is the formal way to discuss important matters democratically.

What is needed: 1 in 5 signatures from all members (minimum 20% of total membership).

Step 5: Collective Decision Making

At the meeting, all members vote on important matters. This happens fairly and anonymously. This is how we shape our future together.

Result: A mosque with full transparency and trust from all members.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ Is this fitna (conflict)?

Answer: No! Transparency is not conflict. Asking questions and being honest is an Islamic duty. Fitna happens when we are dishonest and hide things. Clarity and trust prevent fitna.

🔐 Is the voting anonymous?

Answer: Yes! We use voting slips so no one can see who you voted for. This gives everyone safety. You can vote what you really believe, without pressure or fear.

💰 What happens with donations?

Answer: All donations go to the mosque. We use the money for: building, electricity, lessons, activities and helping members in need. Everything is recorded and you can find it in the annual report.

👥 Can all members vote?

Answer: Yes! All members have voting rights. This is in the bylaws. Your vote is just as important as everyone else's. This is called 'Shura' - consulting together.

📑 How can I access documents?

Answer: If you want to know more, you can download the bylaws below. You can also contact the board. All members have the right to information.
